The world’s largest market for electric cars is getting serious. 

Those skeptical of China’s booming electric-vehicle sales say they’ve been built on hefty subsidies and other policy support—and that the market will tank once Beijing taps the brakes. But the naysayers have been proven wrong so far. Even though Beijing cut subsidies for electric-vehicle makers by as much as 40% during last year and imposed tougher technological standards, Chinese electric-car sales rose more than 80% from a year earlier in November.
