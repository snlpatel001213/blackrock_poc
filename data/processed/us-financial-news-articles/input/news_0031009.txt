For nearly three decades, U.S. employers have been able to hire highly skilled workers from other countries using the federal H-1B visa program.
More than 2.5 million new visas have been issued since the program was introduced in 1990, with the annual number ranging from 76,627 to 136,890 over the last decade, or roughly one-tenth of 1% of the U.S. civilian workforce.
