Regarding Fred Ehrman’s letter “The Bard Prophesied a Wolff” (Jan. 11): The reference to Macbeth is more apt than the letter writer realizes. Macbeth convinces himself that he deserves to be king and that his ambitions justify telling lies, committing and covering up crimes, and putting his country at risk. The speech is made when he realizes his ambitions haven’t brought him the rewards he expected, and that things keep going wrong.
