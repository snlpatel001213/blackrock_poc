Regarding Rebecca Johnson’s “If You Want Women to Move Up, You Have to Accommodate Mothers” (op-ed, Jan. 5): Why does this argument keep coming up? Children have gone to work with their parents for thousands of years: to the fields, to the shops and the throne room. We learned hard work, negotiation and leadership.
Today we have a society mired in a drug crisis, with failing schools and young people in doubt about the grown-ups running the country. That’s because we don’t let them inside our world. We have shut them out....
