TOKYO—Japan’s trade surplus fell sharply in 2017 despite record high exports to China and Asia, while its surplus with the U.S., which has been under close scrutiny in Washington, edged up amid growing global trade tensions.
The overall surplus fell 25% to ¥2.99 trillion ($27.1 billion), as faster growth in imports—notably energy—outweighed hefty gains in exports, data from the country’s finance ministry showed on Wednesday.
