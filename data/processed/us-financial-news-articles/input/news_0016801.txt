January 12, 2018 / 2:09 PM / Updated 38 minutes ago Canada welcomes Trump suggestion of NAFTA deadline extension   
LONDON, Ontario (Reuters) - Canada welcomes the suggestion by U.S. President Donald Trump that the deadline for concluding talks to modernize NAFTA could be extended, Foreign Minister Chrystia Freeland told reporters on Friday. FILE PHOTO - Canada's Foreign Minister Chrystia Freeland attends a news briefing in Kiev, Ukraine December 21, 2017. REUTERS/Valentyn Ogirenko 
Trump told the Wall Street Journal on Thursday that “a lot of things are hard to negotiate” ahead of a Mexican presidential election later this year. Talks to update the North American Free Trade Agreement are due to wrap up by end-March. Reporting by David Ljunggren
