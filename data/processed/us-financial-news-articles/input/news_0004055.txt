LONDON (Reuters) - Unilever ( ULVR.L )( UNc.AS ) plans to close its Colman’s mustard factory in the English city of Norwich next year, affecting 113 jobs, the consumer goods giant said on Thursday. FILE PHOTO: The logo of the Unilever group is seen at the Miko factory in Saint-Dizier, France, May 4, 2016. REUTERS/Philippe Wojazer/File Photo 
About 40 of the jobs will transfer to the English city of Burton upon Trent, where Marmite and Bovril are made, and about 25 will be created by a new facility in Norwich for production and packing of Colman’s mustard powder, mustard milling and mint processing, Unilever said.
