Brendan Byrne, a two-term New Jersey governor who legalized gambling in the state and served as an elder statesman for decades, died Thursday.
Mr. Byrne, 93 years old, died at a home in Livingston, N.J. with his wife by his side, his son Tom Byrne said. Mr. Byrne came down with a cold over the holidays that turned flu-like and caused respiratory problems, his son said.
