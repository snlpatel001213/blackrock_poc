TOKYO—Tatsuro Toyoda, the former Toyota Motor Corp. president who led the company’s climb to become one of the world’s top auto makers, has died. He was 88.
Mr. Toyoda, a son of the company’s founder, died Dec. 30 of pneumonia, the Japanese auto maker said Saturday.
