House passes FISA bill after Trump tweet sparks confusion Friday, January 12, 2018 - 01:46 
The U.S. House of Representatives on Thursday passed a bill to renew the National Security Agency’s warrantless internet surveillance program, overcoming objections from privacy advocates and confusion prompted by morning tweets from President Donald Trump that initially questioned the spying tool. Andy Sullivan reports.
