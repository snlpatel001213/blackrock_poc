OTTAWA—Joshua Boyle, a Canadian man who was held hostage with his American wife and family for five years in Afghanistan and Pakistan before being rescued in October, was charged in court with sexual assault and other offenses.
Mr. Boyle, appearing before an Ottawa courtroom on Wednesday, also faced charges of misleading police, unlawful confinement, threatening death and forcing his victim to ingest the antidepressant drug Trazodone, which court documents described as a “noxious thing.”
