Are human beings essentially pure in heart or rotten to the core? Philosophers have debated this question for millennia. In the fourth and third centuries B.C., Mencius argued that “benevolence is the distinguishing characteristic of man,” while his fellow Confucian Xunzi retorted: “People’s nature is bad.”
