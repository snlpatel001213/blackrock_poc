Zimbabwe, once the jewel of Africa, may be regaining some of its luster. Though it’s easy to be cynical about regime change in Africa, President Emmerson Mnangagwa, sworn in Nov. 24, appears to mean business.

It’s been a long time coming. For decades Zimbabwe’s people suffered through miserable privation. Former President Robert Mugabe, who ruled from 1980 until he was deposed last year, treated the country’s assets like he was at an all-you-can-eat buffet.
