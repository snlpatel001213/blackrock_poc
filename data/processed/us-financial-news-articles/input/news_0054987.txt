New York City Mayor Bill de Blasio said he will speed up an initiative to outfit police officers with body cameras—a move that could increase tensions between his administration and the police union.
All of the New York Police Department’s roughly 18,000 officers and detectives on patrol will wear body cameras by the end of this year, the mayor said Tuesday at a news conference in Brooklyn. Previously, Mr. de Blasio had said they would receive the cameras by the end of 2019.
