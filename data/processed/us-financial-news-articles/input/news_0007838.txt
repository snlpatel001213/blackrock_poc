WASHINGTON—The House Intelligence Committee is expecting soon to have a detailed look at the activities of the research firm behind a dossier on President Donald Trump that has become a flashpoint in the investigations into Russia interference during the 2016 election.
In twin victories this week, the Republican-run committee won access to banking records and federal law-enforcement material related to work performed by Fusion GPS, a company hired during the 2016 campaign to conduct research on Mr. Trump’s ties to Russia.
