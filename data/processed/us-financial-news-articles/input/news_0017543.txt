The most obvious thing to say about “The Commuter” is that it’s a train wreck; many will say that, and they’ll be right. What’s less obvious at first, though increasingly clear as this thrill-free thriller chugs on, is that the plot is entirely unnecessary—not just another Hitchcock rip-off with logical lapses, but absurd from start to finish. There’s no reason why someone else couldn’t have done, with much less fuss, what Liam Neeson’s insurance salesman is forced to do in the course of heading home from Grand Central Terminal aboard a Metro-North train.
