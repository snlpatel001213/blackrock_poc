Here’s what Bill Whitman, a 62-year-old advertising agent, says about his wife in the title story of Denis Johnson’s collection “The Largesse of the Sea Maiden” (Random House, 207 pages, $27): “She’s petite, lithe, quite smart; short gray hair, no makeup. A good companion. At any moment—the very next second—she could be dead.”
