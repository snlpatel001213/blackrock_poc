For people scared away from poetry by bad high-school teaching, or a fear of missing the “hidden” meanings lurking beneath the surface, here’s a piece of advice: Do not worry. Poetry need not be hard.
Appreciating it requires only two things: an eye for figures of speech (saying something a little slant); and an ear for music, rhythms and forms. Accessible poems can be deep; seemingly complex ones can be shallow.
