AT&T Inc., a company that has had virtually nothing to do with show business for most of its history, now faces a court battle that’s all about it.
Early signs suggest the antitrust fight over AT&T’s $85 billion takeover of Time Warner Inc. will focus heavily on the small screen, with much of its evidence coming from the companies’ video rivals. Those competitors argue the telecom company will use Time Warner’s entertainment assets against them, according to documents reviewed by The Wall Street Journal.
