I feel reasonably certain that when he set out to write his true-crime page-turner “The Girl on the Velvet Swing,” Simon Baatz had no inkling of how relevant it would be upon publication. At a moment when the relationship between an artist’s moral character and the value of his work has become a subject of heated debate, this vivid retelling of the 1906 murder of Stanford White couldn’t be timelier.
