James Panero’s “A Brief History of Idol-Smashers, From Moses to de Blasio” (op-ed, Jan. 20) regarding vandalizing memorials makes a greater point about divisive racial politics than meets the eye. New York Mayor Bill de Blasio’s commission targets the statue of J. Marion Sims, the “father of gynecology.” Sims is accused of experimenting on enslaved women, performing repeated surgical procedures without anesthesia and financially profiting from the results of his experiments.
