SEOUL—South Korea stepped away from a potential clash with Japan over wartime history, saying it would abide by a 2015 agreement over women forced into sexual service for Japanese soldiers.
With the move, President Moon Jae-in sought to conciliate a neighbor whose cooperation is essential to countering the threat from North Korea’s nuclear and missile programs. Still, his government reiterated concerns about the agreement, sparking a formal protest from Tokyo and suggesting tensions could flare up again.
