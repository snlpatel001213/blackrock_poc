Andrew Carnegie By David Nasaw (2006)
1. Scotsman Andrew Carnegie entered the U.S. in the mid-19th century, age 12, penniless and undereducated but bristling with ambition. David Nasaw captures in fine detail Carnegie’s transformation from precocious office assistant and telegraph operator at the Pennsylvania Railroad to richest man in the world—and America’s happy tycoon.
