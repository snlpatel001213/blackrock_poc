Two Italians and five U.S. residents have been charged with operating pain clinics in Florida and Tennessee that illicitly distributed painkillers linked to a “significant percentage” of 700 deaths, the Justice Department said Friday.
The charges in federal court came in a long-running investigation that has resulted in the convictions of more than 100 drug dealers who purchased and distributed drugs obtained at pain clinics the Justice Department described in court papers as “pill mills.”
