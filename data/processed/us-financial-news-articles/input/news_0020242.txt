I have a friend who replies to my texts with her own humorless Ha Code.
A single “ha” conveys zero joviality. It’s basically a put-down. I write: “You really gobbled up that second dessert,” and she replies, “ha.” A double ha—“haha”—makes it seem as if she’s at least mildly amused, but she’s not. I write: “There are three Starbucks within seven blocks of my office, and they’re all busy all the time.” She replies, “haha,” but she means, “So what?”
