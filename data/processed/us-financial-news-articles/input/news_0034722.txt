The best novels, with only a small number of notable exceptions—“Don Quixote,” “In Search of Lost Time,” “Ulysses”—have been family novels. “War and Peace,” “Anna Karenina,” “The Brothers Karamazov,” “My Antonia,” “The Brothers Ashkenazi,” “Buddenbrooks,” “Joseph and His Brothers,” family novels all, provide a deeper pleasure than does most other fiction. Family, as William Shakespeare (author of “King Lear,” “Hamlet” and “Macbeth”) would have been the first to tell you, is the great literary subject.
