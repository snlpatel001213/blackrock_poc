The new year is less than a week old, and already Europe is staging another collective freak-out over elections. The fright that started in 2016 with Britain’s vote to leave the European Union and then America’s choice of Donald Trump abated in 2017 after Dutch, French and German voters favored more conventional politicians. Now Italian, Czech, Hungarian and even Swedish elections in 2018 are reviving fears of a populist surge.
