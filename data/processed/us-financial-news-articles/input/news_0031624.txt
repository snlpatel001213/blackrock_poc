The U.S. dollar rose Wednesday after Apple Inc. said it would repatriate earnings from overseas, signaling a substantial source of demand for the currency.
The Wall Street Journal Dollar Index, which measures the currency against a basket of others, rose 0.3% to 84.71. It was the biggest jump for the currency since Nov. 20. The dollar rose 0.6% against the euro, its biggest gain since Oct. 26.
