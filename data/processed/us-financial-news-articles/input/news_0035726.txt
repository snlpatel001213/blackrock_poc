WASHINGTON—Federal Reserve nominee Marvin Goodfriend told lawmakers Tuesday he supports the central bank’s dual mandate to maintain stable prices and pursue maximum employment, after sharp questioning from Democrats over his past comments suggesting the Fed should focus on inflation.
Democrats pressed Mr. Goodfriend at his confirmation hearing to explain his warnings following the financial crisis that the Fed should move quickly to raise interest rates, including remarks in 2012 in which he said inflation could begin to rise...
