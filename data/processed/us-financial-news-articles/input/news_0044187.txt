“Most eyes are on Italy’s upcoming election, but economists also are focusing on an Italian mystery,” Marcello Minenna writes for The Wall Street Journal. “The country has made considerable progress cleaning up the problems in the financial system that seemed to be holding back the economy, and the economy is growing again. Yet that growth seems to have little relationship to the newfound health of the banks, and no one can quite say what the breaking of this link means for the future.”
