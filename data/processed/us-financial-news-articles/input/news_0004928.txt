GHAZZE, Lebanon—It was the middle of harvest season when government soldiers snatched Ghada Abo Mestu’s husband from the apple orchard of his family farm in Syria. One bashed his head with a rifle butt before he was dragged away.
That was in 2012, more than a year into Syria’s civil war. Since then, Ms. Mestu has devoted much of her life to trying to find him—pleading with security officials for information, meeting with released prisoners, combing through grisly photographs of those who have died in detention.
