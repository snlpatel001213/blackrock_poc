One of the few useful reforms after the 2008 financial panic added a modicum of market discipline for money-market funds. Wouldn’t you know, Republicans on Capitol Hill want to dismantle it. 

That’s the message of a bill that cleared the House Financial Services Committee on Jan. 18, on a 34-21 vote. The bill would let institutional prime and tax-exempt money-market mutual funds once again hide behind a $1 NAV, or net-asset value.
