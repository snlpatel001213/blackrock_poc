Gold prices rose for the eighth straight session Tuesday, supported by a weaker dollar.
Gold for February delivery rose 0.4% to $1,314.40 a troy ounce on the Comex division of the New York Mercantile Exchange. The precious metal just posted its best year since 2010, boosted by the dollar falling and geopolitical tensions, and was on track for a 12th day of gains in the last 13 sessions to start 2018. Prices are at their highest level since September.
