New York
Grass green, aubergine, teal and the madder red of tribal rugs—these are the wall colors that form the hushed background for “Veiled Meanings: Fashioning Jewish Dress, From the Collection of the Israel Museum, Jerusalem.” There is no sound in the large first-floor gallery of the Jewish Museum, no video screens to distract us from the hundred or so articles of clothing at hand, dating primarily from the 1800s to the early 1900s.
