When the teenage Fred Bass began working at his father’s store in the early 1940s, it was among dozens of used booksellers on a stretch of Manhattan’s Fourth Avenue known as Book Row. As rents increased, nearly all of them were heading for oblivion.
The father, Benjamin Bass, an immigrant from Lithuania who founded the Strand book store in 1927, was ready to close the shop in the mid-1950s and switch to a mail-order business.
