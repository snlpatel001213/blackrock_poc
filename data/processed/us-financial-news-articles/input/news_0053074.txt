January 29, 2018 / 12:44 PM / Updated 8 minutes ago Wall St. slides as Apple weighs Reuters Staff 1 Min Read 
NEW YORK (Reuters) - Wall Street pulled back from record highs on Monday with the S&P 500 marking its biggest one-day percentage decline in about five months, weighed down by a slide in Apple shares. 
The Dow Jones Industrial Average .DJI fell 177.23 points, or 0.67 percent, to 26,439.48, the S&P 500 .SPX lost 19.34 points, or 0.67 percent, to 2,853.53 and the Nasdaq Composite .IXIC dropped 39.27 points, or 0.52 percent, to 7,466.51. Reporting by Stephen Culp; Editing by Nick Zieminski
