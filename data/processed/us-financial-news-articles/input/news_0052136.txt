Tencent Holdings Ltd., China’s largest company, is making a big splash in the shopping mall rental market. Its biggest return could come from renting out its name. 
The tech giant is part of a group including e-commerce firm JD.com, electrical appliance retailer Suning and property developer Sunac China Holdings Ltd. that will invest a whopping $5.4 billion for a 14% stake into Dalian Wanda Commercial Properties—China’s largest mall operator and a subsidiary of Dalian Wanda Group.
