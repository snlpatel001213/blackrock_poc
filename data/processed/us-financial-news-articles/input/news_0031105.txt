New York hedge fund Soroban Capital Partners plans to shrink its main fund by $1.5 billion after a year of double-digit gains, becoming the latest in a string of managers to return some client money as stock markets have rallied.
The $11.1 billion stock-picking firm told its clients in a letter last week that it remained “optimistic” about the investment environment but that the planned return of money from its oldest fund “allows for the continued flexibility to compound capital over the long run.”
