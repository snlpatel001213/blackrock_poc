James Rollins’s riveting “The Demon Crown” (Morrow, 441 pages, $28.99) is a mix of biology, techno-thriller and archaeology. You have to hope that what you’re reading is fiction.
Much of it isn’t. The biology element centers on insects, the most dangerous creatures on earth—especially one group of them that seriously bothered Darwin—and on venomics, the study of toxins.
