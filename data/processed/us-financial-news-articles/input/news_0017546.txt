The sixth iteration of the Prototype Festival, the annual celebration of new work in opera theater, which runs through Jan. 20, began with some multimedia world premieres. The chamber version of Michael Gordon’s clever “Acquanetta,” which opened on Tuesday at the Gelsey Kirkland Arts Center in Brooklyn, fits in with the #TimesUp zeitgeist of the Golden Globes: It is about the objectification of women in cinema.
