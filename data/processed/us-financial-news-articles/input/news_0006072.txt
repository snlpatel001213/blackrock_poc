BERLIN (Reuters) - EasyJet ( EZJ.L ) aims to more than double passenger numbers in Germany to 18 million this year after taking over some of failed airline Air Berlin’s operations at Berlin’s Tegel airport, a manager at the British budget carrier said. An EasyJet placard reading "Finlay" is seen a Tegel airport in Berlin before an event of the British budget carrier EasyJet to present the first flight from airport Tegel in Berlin, Germany January 5, 2018. REUTERS/Hannibal Hanschke 
Europe managing director Thomas Haagensen was speaking on Friday to mark easyJet’s first flight from Tegel, where it will compete with Lufthansa ( LHAG.DE ) on German domestic routes to cities including Munich and Frankfurt. 
EasyJet already flies from Berlin’s other airport, Schoenefeld, to destinations outside of Germany. Reporting by Klaus Lauer; Writing by Maria Sheahan and Mark Potter
