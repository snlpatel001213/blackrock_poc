The U.S. homeownership rate rose in 2017 for the first time in 13 years as young households overcame significant hurdles to purchase homes in growing numbers and one of the deepest wounds from the housing crisis began to heal.
The annual increase marks a crucial turning point because it comes without a significant push from the federal government, which has retreated from policies that encouraged Americans to buy homes during the previous housing boom but ultimately helped lead to the collapse of an overinflated market.
