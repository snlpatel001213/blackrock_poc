SEOUL—A fire tore through a hospital in South Korea early Friday, killing nearly 40 people and injuring dozens more, authorities said.
The country’s deadliest blaze in a decade began around 7:35 a.m. in an emergency room on the first floor of the Sejong Hospital in the southeast city of Miryang, Choi Man-wu, head of the Miryang fire department, told a televised briefing.
