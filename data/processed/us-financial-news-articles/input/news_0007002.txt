As protests rattled Iran this week, Tehran’s main rival in the region has stayed unusually quiet.
Unlike the U.S. and Israel, which have publicly supported the demonstrations in Iran, officials in Saudi Arabia have kept mum. The street protests of largely working-class Iranians represent the boldest challenge in years to Iran’s ruling elite, with which Saudi Arabia and other Sunni Muslim monarchies are locked in proxy wars across the Middle East.
