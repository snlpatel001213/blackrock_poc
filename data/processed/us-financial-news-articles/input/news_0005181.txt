‘I’m a very long story,” a Palestinian defense attorney tells a man who’s all eyes for her beauty, and all ears for whatever she’s willing to tell him about herself. She’s also a very good story, one of three at the center of “In Between,” a fascinating debut feature by Maysaloun Hamoud. The setting is contemporary Tel Aviv, where the lawyer, Laila (Mouna Hawa), shares an apartment with two other Palestinian women. They’re no strangers to cool glances or daily prejudice, but their immediate conflict isn’t with Israelis, or Israel.
