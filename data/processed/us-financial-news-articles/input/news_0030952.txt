ONE MAN’S TRASH is another man’s empire—at least for John Derian. The designer known for transforming 19th-century ephemera into quirkily timeless home goods now has three shops in Manhattan and a fourth opening in the spring. But in the beginning, it was trash.

“My obsession with paper started when I was 20, at a flea market in Salem, where a dealer was getting rid of his business box by box,” said Mr. Derian, 55, who grew up in Watertown, Mass. “I couldn’t believe the prints were so old because they were so bright and colorful.”...
