JOHANNESBURG—South African President Jacob Zuma said a commission of inquiry would investigate allegations that he allowed the Gupta family to hold undue sway over his government, a day before his own party is set to discuss whether to force his resignation.
Tuesday’s announcement, which came more than a year after the country’s official corruption watchdog ordered the creation of such a commission, is the latest sign that Mr. Zuma’s grip on Africa’s largest economy is weakening in the midst of the largest political scandal...
