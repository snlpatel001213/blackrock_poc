PRAGUE—Czech voters are set to choose a president, Friday and Saturday, in a tensely fought contest that reflects the region’s crosscurrents of pro-Russian nativism and Western liberalism.

President Milos Zeman is seeking re-election in a bid to move the country closer to Moscow—and away from the European Union, which he accuses of plotting an “organized invasion” of Muslims into Europe.
