Few incoming prime ministers face quite as daunting a challenge as Mateusz Morawiecki. The new Polish leader, a former chief executive of one of the country’s largest banks who took over in December having previously served as finance minister, inherits a thriving economy, robust public finances and a 20-percentage point opinion poll lead. 

Yet he also inherits one of the potentially most explosive situations in Europe: the European Commission last month launched a formal procedure that could lead to Poland’s being suspended...
