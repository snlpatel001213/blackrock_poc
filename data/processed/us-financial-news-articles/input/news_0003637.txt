A digital database of centuries-old books from the dawn of printing is shedding new light on the power of the printed word.
Since 2010, University of Oxford researcher Cristina Dondi and her team of antique book experts have carried out a vast and unusual treasure hunt: tracking down and cataloging the half-million books printed from 1450 to 1500 that still survive and are now scattered across 4,000 libraries in Europe and the U.S.
