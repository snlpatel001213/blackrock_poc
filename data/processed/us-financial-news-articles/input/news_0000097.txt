Regarding your editorial “The Uncharitable Charities” (Dec. 26): The editorial board’s claim that charities disrespect their donors by assuming that “most donate mainly because of the tax break” appears to contradict the Journal’s long-held tenet that if you want more of something, tax it less. Certainly every charity I give to sells itself on its merit. However, as I consider my net outlays, deductibility of the contemplated donation certainly influences its size. This probable change in behavior is what worries charities.
