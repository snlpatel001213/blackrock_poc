WASHINGTON—In high-stakes negotiations over immigration, the Trump administration is pushing for policy changes that limit the rights of asylum seekers and unaccompanied children who cross the U.S. border, as well as for more money for the president’s promised border wall.
Democrats oppose all these elements, but with the clock ticking toward a possible government shutdown, people in both parties said that talks Thursday had a more positive tone than in recent days.
