Beijing may be getting tough on China’s overleveraged financial sector, but one corner seems unscathed: the country’s listed insurance companies.
In the latest good news, China Life—the country’s biggest listed insurer and one of the world’s largest by premiums—said Wednesday its premium income for 2017 would top 500 billion yuan ($80 billion), up almost 20% on the year before.
