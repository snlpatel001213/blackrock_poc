LONDON—British Prime Minister Theresa May shook up her cabinet Monday but kept most of her senior ministers in place, in a sign of her limited authority after losing her party’s majority in a failed election gamble in 2017.
Monday’s moves didn’t alter the delicate balance within Mrs. May’s cabinet between those in favor of a decisive break with the European Union and those wanting to maintain close ties with the bloc.
