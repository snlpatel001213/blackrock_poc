Warren Buffett once quipped that he makes “more money when I am snoozing than when I am active.”
General Electric’s new boss John Flannery certainly can’t adopt a Rip Van Winkle management strategy atop the troubled conglomerate, but he should consider a less-frenetic pace of corporate transformation than his predecessor. Pressure to please the market has cost GE’s shareholders a tremendous amount of money—a malady that affects much of corporate America.
