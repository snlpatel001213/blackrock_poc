Tesla Inc. said it updated the pay package for Chief Executive Elon Musk with a plan that again ties his compensation entirely to key performance benchmarks—albeit this time much larger ones, including an ultimate goal to hit $650 billion in market value.
The pay plan, which shareholders must still approve, is designed to keep Mr. Musk as Tesla’s top leader for years to come—although allows for the prospect that he might evolve away from the day-to-day role of CEO into one of executive chairman and chief product officer.
