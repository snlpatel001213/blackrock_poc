The dollar edged higher Friday after a meeting between a Democratic leader in Congress and President Donald Trump raised hopes of sufficient progress in negotiations to avert a government shutdown.
The Wall Street Journal Dollar Index, which measures the currency against a basket of 16 others, gained for the third time in the last four sessions, rising 0.1% to 84.51. On a weekly basis, the index fell for the sixth consecutive time, the longest losing streak since the week ending Feb. 3, 2017.
