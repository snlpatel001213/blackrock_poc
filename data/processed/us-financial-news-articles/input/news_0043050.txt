The agreement to bring athletes from North and South Korea together at next month’s Winter Olympics shows economic pressure on Pyongyang is working and represents an opportunity to deepen the dialogue between the countries, South Korea’s Foreign Minister said Thursday.
“The calculations may have changed on the part of North Korea,“ said Kang Kyung-wha. ”Trade across the border with China has largely frozen up. There is mounting observation and evidence that the sanctions are having an effect.”
