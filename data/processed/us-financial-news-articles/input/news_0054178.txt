A late-18th century palace that was built for a Hungarian count then fell into ruin before getting a 21st-century reboot, has come on the market for $4 million.

The 9,500-square-foot columned structure in eastern Budapest was rescued by a local restaurateur who spent $1.4 million to restore the building with lavish materials and traditional techniques.
