(Reuters) - Southwest Airlines Co ( LUV.N ) on Thursday reported a quarterly profit that more than tripled, helped by a $1.4 billion benefit due to tax reform legislation in the quarter. 
The fourth-largest U.S. airline by passenger traffic said net income rose to $1.89 billion , or $3.18 per share, in the quarter ended Dec. 31, from $522 million, or $0.84 per share, a year earlier. [nPn9MH4dwa] 
Operating revenue rose 3.9 percent to $5.27 billion. Reporting by Sanjana Shivdas in Bengaluru; Editing by Supriya Kurane
