January 30, 2018 / 9:34 PM / in 14 minutes Shutterfly to buy photography company Lifetouch for $825 million Reuters Staff 1 Min Read 
(Reuters) - Online retailer Shutterfly Inc said on Tuesday it would buy privately-held photography company Lifetouch for $825 million in cash. 
Shares of Shutterfly were up 15.3 percent at $61.5 in extended trading. 
The company said the acquisition is estimated to add about $935 million to its revenue and about $100 million to its adjusted EBITDA in 12 months following the closure of the deal, which is expected in the second quarter. Reporting by Munsif Vengattil in Bengaluru; Editing by Anil D'Silva
