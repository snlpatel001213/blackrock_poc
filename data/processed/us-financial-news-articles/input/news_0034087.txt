First round: beat Victor Estrella Burgos (Dominican Republic) 6-1 6-1 6-1

Spanish top seed Nadal has dropped only one set on his way to the quarter-finals, recording dominant victories in the first three rounds before overcoming a feisty challenge from Diego Schwartzman in the last 16. 
The world number one has won five consecutive matches against Cilic, a run that started with him defeating the Croat in the last 16 at Melbourne Park in 2011. 6-MARIN CILIC 
ATP ranking: 6 (Highest ranking: 4) 
Grand slam titles: 1 (U.S. Open 2014) 
2017 Australian Open performance: Second round 
Best Australian Open performance: Semi-finals 2010 
2017 ATP win-loss record: 44-22 
2018 Australian Open results: 
First round: beat Vasek Pospisil (Canada) 6-2 6-2 4-6 7-6(5) 
Second round: beat Joao Sousa (Portugal) 6-1 7-5 6-2 
Third Round: beat Ryan Harrison (U.S.) 7-6(4) 6-3 7-6(4) 
Fourth Round: beat Pablo Carreno Busta (Spain) 6-7(2) 6-3 7-6(0) 7-6(3) 
Cilic has lost only two sets en route to the quarter-finals and celebrated his 100th grand slam victory when he ground down Spain’s Carreno Busta. 
The former U.S. Open champion beat Nadal once before, in the China Open semi-finals in 2009, but the Spaniard will offer the Croatian the toughest test of his title credentials he has faced in Melbourne.
