RIO DE JANEIRO—Brazil’s Workers’ Party on Thursday proclaimed former President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva its candidate in the October presidential election despite a court ruling that confirmed his conviction for corruption and put a significant legal hurdle on his path to the presidency.
Top party leaders, meeting in São Paulo, called for demonstrations and civil disobedience before hundreds of red-clad supporters under a banner reading “in defense of democracy and of Lula,” as Mr. da Silva is universally known.
