This season, Carnegie Hall is turning back the clock a half-century. But its goal is actually to find an audience for the future.
The hall is presenting a two-month festival called “The ’60s” that looks at the pivotal decade through a series of not just concerts, but also lectures, films and dance and theatrical events. Participating artists and groups include singer-songwriter David Crosby, the Dance Theatre of Harlem and the Kronos Quartet.
