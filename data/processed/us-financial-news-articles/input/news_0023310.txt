“Drilling Plan Eases Rules” (page one, Dec. 26) describes the Trump administration’s proposal to decrease the so-called regulatory burden on the oil and gas industry by modifying a highly technical regulation designed to prevent offshore-well blowouts like the 2010 BP Deepwater Horizon tragedy that killed 11 workers and spewed approximately 200 million gallons of oil. A top-down, administration-wide approach to reduce regulations and/or postpone implementation dates inevitably will result in preventable injuries, fatalities and adverse environmental impacts.
