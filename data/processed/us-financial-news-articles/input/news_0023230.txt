It seems fitting that the untimely death of Peter Sutherland this week should have occurred on the eve of the 100th anniversary of President Woodrow Wilson’s 14 Points speech.
The former Irish politician served as the European Union’s competition commissioner during the 1980s when the rules for the EU single market were set, and then as the founding director general of World Trade Organization in the 1990s.
