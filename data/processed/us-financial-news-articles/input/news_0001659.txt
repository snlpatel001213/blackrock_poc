The Metropolitan Opera’s new “Tosca,” which opened on New Year’s Eve, was intended as a corrective. In 2009, for the opening night of Peter Gelb’s fourth season as general manager, the company replaced its beloved 1985 Franco Zeffirelli production, a lavish, hyper-realistic depiction of the opera, with a stark, minimalist, more sexually explicit version directed by Luc Bondy.
