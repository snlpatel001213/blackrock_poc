A runaway fish-salting shed in Maine has sparked a border incident.
The problem started when a powerful snowstorm slammed the East Coast on Jan. 4 and ripped a historic “brining shed” from its worm-eaten pilings in Lubec, Maine, the state’s easternmost town. The roughly 75-foot-long wooden building hung around the town’s shore for a couple of days, then floated off for a clam flat on Canada’s Campobello Island.
