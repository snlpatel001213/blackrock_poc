(Reuters) - Amgen Inc said on Friday that the U.S. Food and Drug Administration approved its drug, Xgeva, to prevent fractures in patients with multiple myeloma. FILE PHOTO: An Amgen sign is seen at the company's office in South San Francisco, California October 21, 2013. REUTERS/Robert Galbraith/File Photo 
Xgeva has already been approved to treat a condition in which the calcium level in blood is above normal and giant cell tumor of the bone. 
Amgen reacquired sales rights to Xgeva along with two other drugs from British drugmaker GlaxoSmithKline in 2015.( reut.rs/2F43mf5 ) 
The deal gave the drugmaker rights to sell in 48 countries, including Australia as well as markets in Asia, South America and Europe.
