WASHINGTON—The Trump administration has launched a public review of the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau, a move that could lead to a significant overhaul of the agency created after the financial crisis.
Mick Mulvaney, the White House budget chief who is temporarily overseeing the consumer finance regulator, said Wednesday the agency would publish a series of formal requests in coming weeks to gather public comments on its core activities—enforcement, supervision, rulemaking, market monitoring and education.
