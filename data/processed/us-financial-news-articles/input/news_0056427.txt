Dave Seminara’s “No Wall Will Keep Visitors From Overstaying Their Visas” (op-ed, Jan. 18) correctly points out how tourist and other temporary visas are abused. His call for consular officials to be more stringent in issuing visas is appropriate but would probably have minimal impact for the reasons he lists. More effective, however, would be attaching a bar code to each tourist visa, to be scanned on entrance to the U.S. and rescanned on the tourist’s exit, so the immigration service would always know which tourists have overstayed their visas and for how long. This system is used by Israel.
