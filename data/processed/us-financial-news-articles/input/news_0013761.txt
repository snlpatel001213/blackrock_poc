One of the country’s biggest coal companies has a deal to send nearly one-quarter of its exports to Japan, the latest boost from Asian markets that have been helping U.S. coal miners recover from one of their bleakest eras.
Cloud Peak Energy Inc., a Wyoming company that mines the Powder River Basin in the western U.S., will start supplying a power plant in Fukushima prefecture late next year in a deal scheduled to rise to as much as one million tons annually by 2021. That equates to 22% of the company’s current exports.
