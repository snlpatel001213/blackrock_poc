Peter Stewart, a real-estate developer, in the early 1960s was a member of a commission planning to revitalize downtown Dallas as freeways slashed through it. Parks were on the agenda.
Mr. Stewart wanted more than a green space with benches. While on a fishing trip in Arkansas, he hit on the idea of a park dedicated to gratitude. He then led a drive that created Thanks-Giving Square, a rallying point for interfaith dialogues.
