A growing number of companies plan to use some of the savings they expect to realize from the tax overhaul to boost their contributions to employees’ 401(k) retirement-savings accounts.
About a dozen companies, including Visa Inc. and Nationwide Mutual Insurance Co., have announced plans to raise the payments they make to match employee contributions to 401(k) accounts. Others have said they would make a one-time contribution to employees’ retirement plans.
