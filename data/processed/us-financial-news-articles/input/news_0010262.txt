Hong Kong has been kicking itself ever since it lost the world’s largest ever IPO—that of e-commerce giant Alibaba—to New York four years ago. It may finally make amends this year.

Hong Kong’s chief executive Carrie Lam this week openly invited Alibaba to list in the semiautonomous Chinese city—an idea its founder Jack Ma said it would “seriously consider.” Alibaba skipped Hong Kong in 2014 for its record $25 billion float mainly because the city’s market regulator refused to compromise on its “one share, one vote” principle....
