January 31, 2018 / 11:49 AM / Updated 10 hours ago EU decision on EMA move to Amsterdam is done - EU Commissioner Reuters Staff 1 Min Read 
BRUSSELS (Reuters) - The European Union’s decision to move the European Medicines Authority to Amsterdam after Brexit is a done deal, the bloc’s health commissioner said on Wednesday. 
“Sorry but me personally, I think the whole Commission also is not a part of Italian election debates. It will be a crystal clear answer. The Council decision is done,” Vytenis Andriukaitis told a briefing in Brussels. Reporting by Gabriela Baczynska; editing by Philip Blenkinsop
