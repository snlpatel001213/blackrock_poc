In “Stocks Weren’t Made for Social Climbing” (Inside View, Jan. 22), Andy Kessler expresses concern that “corporate social responsibility” (CSR) funds have failed to keep pace with the S&P 500 Index. He’s right. Over the 10 years ending 2017, annual returns on the average large-cap CSR fund were 7.8%, trailing the 8.5% return of the S&P 500 by 0.7%. But actively managed large-cap funds that don’t consider CSR issues performed even worse, earning 7.7% per year and trailing the index by 0.8%.
