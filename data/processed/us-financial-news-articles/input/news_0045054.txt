The U.K. set out its goals Friday for the next stage of negotiations with Brussels over its withdrawal from the European Union, with signs pointing to a handful of potential stumbling blocks that must be overcome by March.
The U.K.’s proposals, presented in a speech by Brexit Secretary David Davis, come against a backdrop of renewed infighting among members of Britain’s ruling Conservative Party over how clean a break to make, highlighting the ongoing uncertainty over the strength of Prime Minister Theresa May’s grip on power.
