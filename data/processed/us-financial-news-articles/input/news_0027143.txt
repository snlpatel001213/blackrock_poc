Glenn Harlan Reynolds is wrong to propose abolishing federal judicial clerkships (“Why Do Federal Judges Need Clerks, Anyway?,” op-ed, Jan. 8). There is no epidemic of sexual harassment by federal judges. Even so, no one says eliminating all workplace subordinates is an effective countermeasure. More odd, the professor claims the Supreme Court’s work load (80 cases per year) is too light to justify four clerks per justice, so he proposes “eliminating law clerks for the lower federal courts.” Per 2016 figures from the U.S. Courts Administrative Office, there are 640 pending cases per district judgeship.
