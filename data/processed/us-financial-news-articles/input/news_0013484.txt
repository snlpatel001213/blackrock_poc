By any reasonable definition, the insult that sets “The Insult” in motion amounts to hate speech. It’s only the beginning, though, of a conflict that escalates from a neighborhood quarrel about a gutter pipe into rageful expressions of grievances going back half a century. Ziad Doueiri’s film, set in Beirut and framed as a courtroom drama, is a parable of life in contemporary Lebanon and the Middle East, where the past can intrude on any dispute.
