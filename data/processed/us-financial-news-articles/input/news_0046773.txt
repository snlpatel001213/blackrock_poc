DAVOS, Switzerland—U.S. Treasury Secretary Steven Mnuchin said the U.S. isn’t concerned with “where the dollar is in the short term,” as he appeared to play down comments he made Wednesday that helped push down the greenback.
Speaking at a briefing at the World Economic Forum in Davos, Switzerland, Mr. Mnuchin was asked about remarks he made about the sharp weakening of the dollar in recent months. In the remarks, he said a “weaker dollar is good for trade,” in comments that pressured the currency.
