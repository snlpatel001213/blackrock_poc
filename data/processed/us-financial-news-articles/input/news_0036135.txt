After a decade of slow growth, corporate chieftains have good reason to feel buoyant.
In the U.S., the economy grew 3% in the third quarter and Federal Reserve officials in December increased their forecast for 2018 growth to 2.5%, up from 2.1% in September. Bulls on Wall Street boosted the market cap for S&P 500 companies last year by 18%, unemployment stood at a 17-year low, and a big tax cut and regulatory rollbacks portend more gains.
