Employees at the Los Angeles Times have voted to unionize, a historic shift for a publication long resistant to organized labor that will likely escalate rising tensions between newsroom workers and management.
The vote to join the NewsGuild-CWA union passed with 248 workers in favor versus 44 against, the NewsGuild said Friday. The formation of the union at the Times comes after months of public posturing from newsroom leadership and pro-union employees, which both waged campaigns to bring staffers over to their side.
