Jan 29 (Reuters) - Canada’s main stock index slumped on Monday, touching a six-week low as energy and other resource shares tumbled alongside commodity prices.
The Toronto Stock Exchange’s S&P/TSX composite index unofficially closed down 144.5 points, or 0.89 percent, at 16,094.72. (Reporting by Leah Schnurr; Editing by James Dalgleish)
