A Quebec jury found three men not guilty on Friday of criminal negligence in the July 2013 oil train derailment in Lac-Mégantic, Quebec, that killed 47 people and destroyed the town’s core.
The trial centered on the three men’s actions leading up to the tragic accident in which a train operated by Montreal Maine & Atlantic Railway Inc., a subsidiary of U.S. operator Rail World Inc., carrying 73-tanker cars filled with oil sourced from North Dakota’s Bakken Shale formation was left unmanned at a crew rest stop.
