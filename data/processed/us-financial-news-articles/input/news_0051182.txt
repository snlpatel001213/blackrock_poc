The Census Bureau has scrapped a plan to overhaul how it asks about race and Hispanic ethnicity in the 2020 Census, after the Trump administration delayed making a decision on the matter in time for the rollout.

As a result, the Census will keep separate questions for race and Hispanic ethnicity instead of combining them. It also will not add a planned category called “Middle Eastern/North African.” Both changes had undergone extensive testing, including a nationally representative survey of 1.2 million households in 2015....
