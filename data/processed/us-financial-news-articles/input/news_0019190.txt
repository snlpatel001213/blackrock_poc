‘Playfair is the most famous man you have never heard of,” Bruce Berkowitz declares of the subject of his biography. After 10 years delving into archives on both sides of the Atlantic, Mr. Berkowitz pictures William Playfair almost as a caped crusader who “appears everywhere,” “knows everyone” and materializes almost incredibly amid “the Industrial Revolution, the French Revolution, the founding of the United States, the birth of modern economics, the Age of Napoleon.”
