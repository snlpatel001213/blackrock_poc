Four men convicted in the hazing death of a Baruch College freshman were sentenced to up to two years behind bars Monday, while their fraternity was banned from the state of Pennsylvania for 10 years and fined more than $110,000.
The freshman, Chun Hsien “Michael” Deng, of Queens, died after a hazing ritual known as the “glass ceiling,” during which he was blindfolded and repeatedly tackled by his Pi Delta Psi fraternity brothers during a retreat in the state’s Poconos region, prosecutors said.
