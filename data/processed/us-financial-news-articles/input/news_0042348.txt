It’s sweet on the inside, bitter on the outside, crunchy, juicy and aromatic, and you can cook every inch of it. Right now, chefs are taking celery in unexpected directions, far beyond the crudité platter.

In California, our biggest celery-producing state, where this member of the parsley family grows year-round, chef and restaurateur Daniel Patterson has dipped into local culinary history to add spark to the stalks.
