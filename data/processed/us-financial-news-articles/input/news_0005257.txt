A New York City charter school run by the United Federation of Teachers doesn’t serve many students with disabilities and English-language learners, according to data released Thursday, an issue for which the union has criticized other charters.
Only 3% of students at the UFT Charter School in Brooklyn were English-language learners in the last school year, far less than the 12% share in the traditional public schools in the area, a state oversight body found.
