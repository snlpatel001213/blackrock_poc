If it were easy to create the conditions for America’s 1980s resurgence, then all Presidents would be as admired as Ronald Reagan. Yesterday this column noted President Donald Trump’s prediction that the U.S. would soon be “hitting fives,” meaning quarterly economic growth of at least 5%. Let’s pause a moment before emblazoning the slogan on red hats. 
Today was a reminder that while it may not be all that difficult to exceed the mediocrity of the Obama era, it’s way too soon to declare the return of Reagan-style growth.
