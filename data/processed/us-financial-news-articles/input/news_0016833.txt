Federal Reserve Bank of Boston President Eric Rosengren said stronger economic growth and a declining unemployment rate, in part resulting from a short-term boost from new tax cuts, justify at least four interest-rate increases this year.
“We’re starting to get further and further away from what we think is a sustainable unemployment rate,” Mr. Rosengren said in an interview. “I don’t want to get to a situation where we have to tighten more quickly.”
