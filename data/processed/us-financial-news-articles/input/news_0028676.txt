U.S. Justice Department lawyers said Friday they would retry the corruption case of New Jersey Sen. Bob Menendez, whose previous trial ended in a deadlocked jury in November, throwing a wrench into an election year for the two-term senator.
Mr. Menendez, a Democrat, and his co-defendant, Salomon Melgen, were indicted for bribery and corruption by two separate grand juries, federal prosecutors said in a court filing Friday. In the filing, they said “an early retrial date is in the best interest of the public.”
