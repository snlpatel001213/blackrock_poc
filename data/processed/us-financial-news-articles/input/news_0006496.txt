SAN JOSE, Calif.—A year ago Nathan Chen established himself as the great American hope for the 2018 Winter Olympics, the elusive singles skater with a legitimate claim to wresting a gold medal from international contenders who had long left U.S. entrants in the dust.
Such was the enthusiasm for the seven quadruple jumps he packed into two fluid, polished programs for record-shattering scores that the men’s free program, rather than the more glamorous women’s event, now holds the prime-time Saturday evening slot in the competition...
