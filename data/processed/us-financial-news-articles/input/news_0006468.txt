That Robert de La Rochefoucauld, descendant of the illustrious François who gave France his “Maxims,” was extremely brave, there is no doubt. As a young member of the Resistance in World War II, he carried out perilous acts of sabotage, blowing up train stations and munitions factories, and killing Nazi officers with guns, knives and (once) with his bare hands. He also escaped several times from German captivity, on one occasion jumping from a moving truck en route to his own execution.
