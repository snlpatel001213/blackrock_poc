Kurt Stocker, 80, an artist from Corrales, N.M., on his 1954 International Harvester R-120 pickup, as told to A.J. Baime. 

In 2009 I was living in Westcliffe, Colo., a town so small the whole population could fit on a 747. Driving home I used to pass this crapped-out pickup with a “for sale” sign, sitting in a field. I finally called the number and agreed to a price on one condition: The owner could drive the truck to my ranch, which was up nasty roads at 9,000 feet elevation. The truck made the trip, so I wrote a check...
