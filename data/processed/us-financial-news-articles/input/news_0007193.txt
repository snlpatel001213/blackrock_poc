When lunch at Minneapolis’s Great Wall restaurant ended with the presentation of a plastic-wrapped dessert, my colleague from Beijing looked puzzled. Fortune cookies aren’t Chinese; a Japanese man in California is believed to have invented them. 

This was my colleague’s first visit to the U.S., and—apart from wondering why a business would name itself after a crumbling border wall—the optimistic tone of the fortunes confused her. They all sounded very American. A tumultuous 20th century left the Chinese people feeling fatalistic....
