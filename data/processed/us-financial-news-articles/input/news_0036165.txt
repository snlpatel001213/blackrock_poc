Robert Frost might well be right when he says that something there is that doesn’t love a wall. Yet we keep on building them. In her clever, many-layered debut novel, Lucy Hughes-Hallett casts a historian’s panoramic eye over human walls of every kind, from the biblical Jericho to postwar Berlin.
