Ian Mortimer is well-known for his nonfiction “Time Traveler’s Guides” to medieval England, Elizabethan England and later periods. They tell us in detail what we could expect if we could go back in time. Now, in “The Outcasts of Time” (Pegasus, 383 pages, $25.95), the author reverses the process. How would a medieval man react if he could skip forward to our era? Would he be impressed? Terrified? Horrified?
