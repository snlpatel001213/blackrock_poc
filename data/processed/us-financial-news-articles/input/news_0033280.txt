In a cynical reading, the best definition of democracy is rule by the people most skilled at pandering for votes. Those who do it best, in the conservative philosopher Michael Oakeshott’s memorable phrase, are able to manufacture “curable grievances.” The cure they promise, naturally, is to be found in electing them to office.
