Regarding James Taranto’s “The Weekend Interview with Morris P. Fiorina: Moderate Voters, Polarized Parties” (Jan. 6): Yes, all three sectors seem to be polarized and elections are won by very narrow margins, but this is the case today in virtually all Western democracies.
What do all these democracies have in common? An intrusive and overpowering government about which the majority of the electorate seems to be in agreement, whether on the right or left. A nation’s real majority is gathered from all sides no matter what...
