Federal judges in North Carolina issued a broad ruling Tuesday that found Republican lawmakers unlawfully gerrymandered the state’s congressional districts to maximize their partisan advantage, a decision that comes as the Supreme Court also is grappling with whether to rein in the practice.
A special three-judge court invalidated the North Carolina map after finding Republicans adopted it for the driving purpose of magnifying the party’s political power beyond its share of the electorate.
