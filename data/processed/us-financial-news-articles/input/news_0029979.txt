BEIJING—China’s Communist Party on Friday signaled support for constitutional changes to allow the creation of a national anticorruption agency with broad authority to supervise public servants.
Beijing had launched preparations for setting up a “national supervisory commission” early last year, a move that some Chinese lawyers and scholars say lacks constitutional basis and sufficient safeguards for civil rights.
