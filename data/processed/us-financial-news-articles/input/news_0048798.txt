When a U.S.-backed ground force led by Syrian Kurds took control of the northern Syrian city of Manbij from Islamic State more than a year ago, Turkey was so alarmed it launched a campaign to block further Kurdish expansion in the border region.
At U.S. request, it agreed to curtail its effort to seize control of Manbij. But as tensions rise between the NATO allies over the Syria war, the city is fast becoming a flashpoint again.
