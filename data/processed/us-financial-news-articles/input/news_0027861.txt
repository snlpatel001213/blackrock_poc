WASHINGTON—The Supreme Court on Thursday postponed the effect of a lower-court ruling that found Republicans in North Carolina unlawfully gerrymandered the state’s congressional districts to maximize their partisan advantage.
The justices, in a brief written order, granted an emergency request by North Carolina legislative leaders to stay the lower court ruling for now, a move that means state lawmakers won’t have to redraw the congressional lines immediately.
