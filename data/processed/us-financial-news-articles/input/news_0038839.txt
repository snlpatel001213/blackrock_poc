Recently, in the midst of renovating my Brooklyn row house’s kitchen, I developed a crush on a slab of stone called pietra del cardosa, a dark blue sandstone with pale whorls like cosmic dust. Inky and imperfect, it struck me as the antidote to the morgue-like expanse of white marble that greeted me each time I scrolled Pinterest for kitchen-design ideas. Never mind that the surface was prone to staining: I had to have it. Messes and marks might happen, but I would proudly call them patina—and enjoy my moody blue counter.
