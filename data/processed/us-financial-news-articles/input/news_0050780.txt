WASHINGTON—Sen. Marco Rubio said late Saturday night that he had fired his chief of staff after learning a day earlier of allegations of improper conduct.
The Florida Republican said he first learned Friday of allegations that his chief of staff, Clint H. Reed, had “violated office policies regarding proper relations between a supervisor and their subordinates,” which Mr. Rubio said led to actions which he believed “amounted to threats to withhold employment benefits.”
