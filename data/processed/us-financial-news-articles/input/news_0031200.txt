BERLIN—The fate of Europe’s longest-serving leader still in office will be sealed Sunday by a fractious political party that has lost half of its voters and used up six leaders in the past 15 years.
Four months after Germany’s center-left Social Democratic Party scored their worst postwar result in a general election, 600 of the party’s delegates will meet to decide whether to forge a ruling alliance with Angela Merkel’s conservatives and usher in the German chancellor’s fourth term in office.
