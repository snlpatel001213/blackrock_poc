Going all the way back to Plato, Confucius and the Bible, writers have talked about the twin poles of human character—virtue and vice. We’re familiar with the extreme examples of each, from Jesus and Gandhi on the one hand, to Nero and Hitler on the other.
But the broad categories of virtue and vice don’t describe most of us very well. While there may be some outliers on either end, most of us fall somewhere in the middle, in that great bulging center of the classic bell curve. Given our mixed characters, we tend to be neither...
