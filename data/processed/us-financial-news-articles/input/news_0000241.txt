More than 20 people were injured Tuesday morning when a seven-alarm fire struck an apartment building in the Bronx, according to Fire Department of New York officials. 
Four of the 23 people injured were brought to Jacobi Medical Center with life-threatening injuries, officials said. The other victims, including one firefighter, suffered minor injuries. Among the injured were nine children. The blaze began at about 5:30 a.m. at 1547 Commonwealth Ave. in the Van Nest neighborhood of the Bronx.
