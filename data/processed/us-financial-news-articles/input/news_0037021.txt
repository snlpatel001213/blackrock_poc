MEXICO CITY, Jan 23 (Reuters) - Mexico’s retailers’ association on Tuesday estimated that sales at stores open for at least a year would rise 5.8 percent this year compared to 2017. 
The group, known as ANTAD, includes retail chains such as Wal-Mart de Mexico and Soriana, said so-called same-store sales rose 4.7 percent in December and 4.5 percent during all of 2017. (Reporting by Sharay Angulo)
