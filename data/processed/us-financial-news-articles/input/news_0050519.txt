When “Ain’t No Mountain High Enough” was released by Tammi Terrell and Marvin Gaye in 1967, the single peaked at No. 19 on Billboard’s pop chart. Then in 1970, after Diana Ross left the Supremes, her version of the song went to No. 1.

Valerie Simpson, the song’s co-writer, Motown arranger Paul Riser, guitarist Eddie Willis, and the Supremes’ Mary Wilson recently looked back on the song’s evolution.
