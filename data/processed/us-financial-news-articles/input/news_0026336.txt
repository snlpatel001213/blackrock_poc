SEOUL—Athletes from North and South Korea will march together under a single flag at the Winter Olympics next month, the South said Wednesday—part of a sweeping agreement on the Games that marks Seoul’s most ardent and public embrace of its nuclear-armed rival in a decade.
The accord comes after a year of rising tensions and exchanges of bellicose rhetoric between Washington and Pyongyang, as North Korea tested a nuclear weapon and ballistic missiles capable of hitting the U.S.
