There’s a frequently reproduced, disconcerting photograph of Willem and Elaine de Kooning made in 1950 by Rudy Burckhardt. Posed on opposite sides of the frame, the couple turn toward each other but look in different directions, separated by dark and light zones that split the image vertically. Willem, in an open-necked work shirt—clearly studio clothes—faces an enormous canvas smudged with what seems to be a preliminary charcoal drawing.
