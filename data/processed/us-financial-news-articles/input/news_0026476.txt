Saudi Arabia on Wednesday said it would inject $2 billion into Yemen’s central bank to stabilize the nation’s currency, as international criticism grew over the kingdom’s role in a war that has killed thousands and ravaged the economy. 
The cash deposit, ordered by Saudi Arabia’s King Salman, follows a sharp fall in Yemen’s currency against the U.S. dollar in recent days. That has raised the cost of crucial goods as more than eight million people—more than a quarter of the population—risk starvation, aid agencies say.
