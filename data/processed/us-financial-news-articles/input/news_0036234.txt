Child Star By Shirley Temple Black (1988)
1.Buoyant bossiness suffuses Shirley Temple Black’s autobiography, published the year she turned 60. The role she convincingly claims for herself is familiar from her movies, which brought cheer to Depression-era America: She’s the brazen, resilient child whose gumption rubbed off on the grown-ups around her—not least Gertrude Temple, whom she portrays as too naive about Hollywood to be a truly ruthless stage mother.
