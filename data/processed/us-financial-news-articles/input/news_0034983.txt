MEXICO CITY--Mexican retailers plan to increase their investments to build new stores and remodel others in 2018, supported by expectations that sales growth will pick up moderately after a slowdown in 2017, an industry group said Tuesday.
Members of the retail association Antad, who operate more than 53,000 stores, plan to invest $3.1 billion this year, up from an estimated $2.8 billion in 2017, the group’s president Vicente Yáñez said.
