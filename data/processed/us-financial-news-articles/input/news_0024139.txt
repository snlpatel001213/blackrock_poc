JOHANNESBURG—A South African court has ordered consulting firm McKinsey & Co. to freeze a 1 billion rand ($81.4 million) payment it received from state electricity company Eskom, saying it may relate “to property and proceeds of unlawful activities.”
The court order, which was authorized on Dec. 14 by the High Court in Pretoria and reviewed by The Wall Street Journal, will be formally served to McKinsey on Wednesday, said Luvuyo Mfaku, a spokesman for South Africa’s National Prosecuting Authority.
