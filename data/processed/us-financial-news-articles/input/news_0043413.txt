WASHINGTON—The Consumer Financial Protection Bureau on Thursday pushed back the effective date of its rule on prepaid cards by one year, to April 2019, while keeping the core of the regulation as written under the agency’s previous Obama-era leadership.
The CFPB said it is delaying the rule so companies have more time to comply, particularly firms making changes to packaging for prepaid cards sold in stores to provide better disclosures of fees and terms.
