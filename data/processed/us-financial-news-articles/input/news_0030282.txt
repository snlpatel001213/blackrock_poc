MEXICO CITY—Mexican President Enrique Peña Nieto has proposed Irene Espinosa, an economist and senior finance ministry official, to fill the vacant position on the Bank of Mexico’s board of governors, according to a notice sent to Mexico’s Congress on Thursday.
If confirmed by the Senate as a deputy governor, as is expected in the coming weeks, Ms. Espinosa would become the first woman to serve on the central bank’s five-member board, which functions as the Bank of Mexico’s interest-rate-setting committee.
