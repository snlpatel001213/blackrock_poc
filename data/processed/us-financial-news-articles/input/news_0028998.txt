WASHINGTON—House Republicans leading an inquiry into Russian meddling in the 2016 election have delayed the testimony of a close confidante to President Donald Trump, days after two other Trump associates refused to answer questions.
Hope Hicks, a longtime aide to Mr. Trump currently serving as White House communications director, was scheduled to appear Friday before the committee. But her appearance was abruptly delayed Thursday at the committee’s request, according to a spokeswoman for the panel.
