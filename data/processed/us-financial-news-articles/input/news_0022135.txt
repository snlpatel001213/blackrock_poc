TRENTON, N.J.—New Jersey Gov. Chris Christie defended his legacy in his gubernatorial farewell speech Tuesday, trumpeting his administration’s record on issues ranging from taxes and the economy to criminal-justice and addiction treatment.
Mr. Christie, who was prevented by term limits from running for re-election, will leave office next week after an eight-year tenure that also was marred by a public-corruption scandal and an unsuccessful presidential bid.
