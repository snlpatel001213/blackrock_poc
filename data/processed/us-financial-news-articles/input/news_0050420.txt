Martin Feldstein (“Stocks Are Headed for a Fall,” op-ed, Jan. 17) seems to be ignoring recent experience when he suggests that U.S. stocks could be headed for a big fall yet interest rates are bound to increase. Following the 2008-09 stock-market meltdown, interest rates were quickly reduced to their zero bound and remained there for almost eight years.
