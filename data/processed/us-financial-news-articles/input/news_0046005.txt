Avi Schick’s argument against state oversight of religious schools is fundamentally flawed (“New York’s Bid to Control Religious Schools,” Houses of Worship, Jan. 19). The Supreme Court decisions he cites don’t support the view that the state has no interest in the quality of education offered in religious schools. Both Pierce v. Society of Sisters and Wisconsin v. Yoder rest on the explicit assumption that children are receiving an adequate secular education.
