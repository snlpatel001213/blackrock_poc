With the rise of reality television, social media and confessional memoir, modern culture swims in a sea of self-disclosure, embracing explicit detail as a path to personal discovery. In “The Art of Mystery,” her book-length critical essay, novelist Maud Casey offers an alternative view, arguing that what isn’t shown and said can often deepen us even more than what’s openly revealed.
