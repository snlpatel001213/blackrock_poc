As a boy in Austria during World War II, Gerhard Andlinger was hungry enough to steal sweet potatoes and cold enough to scavenge for coal along railroad tracks. He found a way out when the New York Herald Tribune ran a contest in 1948, inviting high-school students in Europe to write essays entitled “The Kind of World I Would Like to Live In.”
