Reports of the Japanese central bank tapering have been exaggerated.

This week, the Bank of Japan bought a less-than-expected amount of long-dated Japanese government bonds: It announced it would buy ¥190 billion ($1.7 billion) worth rather than an expected ¥200 billion at a scheduled auction. That was enough to rattle the market, as investors conjectured the BOJ had begun tapering its bond purchases by stealth, thereby effectively tightening monetary policy.
