WASHINGTON—Two senior senators said that immigration negotiations in Congress wouldn’t be stalled by an injunction temporarily barring the cancellation of the Obama-era program that shielded hundreds of thousands of young undocumented immigrants from deportation.
Sen. John Cornyn of Texas, the No. 2 Senate Republican, said Wednesday that the Tuesday court ruling didn’t lessen the urgency of reaching an immigration deal on Capitol Hill.
