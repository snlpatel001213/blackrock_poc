KUALA LUMPUR, Jan 22 (Reuters) - Malaysia's gross international reserves rose to $103.0 billion as of Jan. 15, from $102.4 billion on Dec. 29, the central bank said on Monday. Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM) said the reserves were sufficient to finance 7.1 months of retained imports, and were 1.1 times the short-term external debt.
